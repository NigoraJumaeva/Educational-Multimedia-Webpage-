Overview

Directed by: Damien Chazelle

Starring: Miles Teller, J.K. Simmons

Genre: Drama, Music

Runtime: 106 minutes

Awards: 3 Academy Awards, including Best Supporting Actor

Plot Summary

Andrew Neiman is a young, ambitious jazz drummer at Shaffer Conservatory. He joins the top band under the direction of Terence Fletcher, a teacher known for his brutal teaching methods. Fletcher's approach pushes Andrew to the brink of his physical and emotional limits. The film explores the cost of greatness and the danger of obsessive ambition.

Themes and Analysis

  • Perfectionism vs. Abuse: Can cruelty ever be justified for the sake of excellence?
  • Obsession: Andrew’s dream of greatness consumes his relationships and mental health.
  • Mentorship and Power: Fletcher claims extreme pressure produces true talent.

Behind the Scenes

"Chazelle wrote the script from personal experience in jazz school."

The film was shot in 19 days, and J.K. Simmons reportedly suffered minor injuries during production. Bradshaw, P. (2015, January 15).The Guardian.
